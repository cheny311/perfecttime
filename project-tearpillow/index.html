1 — Concept

Interaction

When one person squeezes the device, water is pumped into a suspended container, from which it begins to drip onto the body or pillow of the person below. The moisture leaves traces on the pillow, allowing the other person to directly experience the presence of someone else’s tears. Through this system, I explore the tears that were never shed, as well as the tears that are hard to respond to. In this process, crying is externalized into a system.

Tears are no longer a private emotional outcome, but an input that can be registered, responded to, and acknowledged.
